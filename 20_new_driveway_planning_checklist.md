# New Driveway Planning Checklist: Everything to Consider Before You Build

Planning a new driveway requires careful consideration of multiple factors, from vehicle needs and budget to materials and contractor selection. This comprehensive checklist guides you through every step of the planning process, ensuring you don't overlook critical details that could affect your driveway's performance and value.

## Step 1 - Define Your Needs

### Vehicle Types and Count

**Current vehicles:**
- [ ] Count daily-use vehicles
- [ ] Measure vehicle dimensions (length, width, weight)
- [ ] Note any oversized vehicles (trucks, SUVs)

**Future considerations:**
- [ ] Plan for vehicle changes (larger/smaller)
- [ ] Consider adult children's vehicles
- [ ] Account for visitor parking needs

### Daily Usage Patterns

**Traffic frequency:**
- [ ] Number of daily trips
- [ ] Peak usage times
- [ ] Turnaround needs (dead-end driveway)

**Special uses:**
- [ ] Trailer parking
- [ ] RV or motorhome storage
- [ ] Boat parking
- [ ] Work vehicle access

### Future Needs (RV, Boat, Extra Parking)

**Anticipate changes:**
- [ ] Retirement plans (RV travel?)
- [ ] Hobby changes (boat, camper)
- [ ] Family growth
- [ ] Home business needs

**Design for flexibility:**
- [ ] Wider driveway for future needs
- [ ] Reinforced sections for heavy loads
- [ ] Expansion possibilities

## Step 2 - Set Your Budget

### Material Cost Ranges

**Per square foot installed:**
- [ ] Gravel: $1-3
- [ ] Asphalt: $3-7
- [ ] Concrete: $4-15
- [ ] Pavers: $10-30
- [ ] Permeable options: $12-30

**Calculate total:**
- [ ] Measure driveway area (length × width)
- [ ] Multiply by material cost
- [ ] Add 10-15% for unexpected costs

### Labor and Installation Costs

**Typically included in per-square-foot pricing, but confirm:**
- [ ] Excavation and grading
- [ ] Base material and compaction
- [ ] Surface material and installation
- [ ] Edging and borders
- [ ] Cleanup and disposal

### Hidden Costs to Plan For

**Additional expenses:**
- [ ] Permits ($50-500)
- [ ] Tree or stump removal
- [ ] Utility relocation
- [ ] Drainage improvements
- [ ] Landscaping restoration
- [ ] Curb cut (if needed): $500-2,000

### Financing Options

**If needed:**
- [ ] Home equity loan
- [ ] Personal loan
- [ ] Contractor financing
- [ ] Credit cards (for smaller projects)
- [ ] Savings plan (delay project)

## Step 3 - Choose Your Material

### Quick Comparison of Options

| Factor | Gravel | Asphalt | Concrete | Pavers |
|--------|--------|---------|----------|--------|
| Cost | $ | $$ | $$$ | $$$$ |
| Lifespan | 100+ yrs* | 15-20 yrs | 25-30 yrs | 25-50 yrs |
| Maintenance | High | Moderate | Low | Moderate |
| Climate | All | Cold | Hot/Mod | All |

*With ongoing maintenance

### Climate Suitability

**Your climate:**
- [ ] Cold with freeze-thaw: Asphalt or pavers
- [ ] Hot with UV exposure: Concrete or pavers
- [ ] Heavy rainfall: Permeable options
- [ ] Coastal: Pavers with marine sealant

### Maintenance Commitment

**Your preference:**
- [ ] Low maintenance: Concrete
- [ ] Moderate maintenance: Asphalt or pavers
- [ ] Don't mind regular work: Gravel

## Step 4 - Design and Layout

### Dimensions and Shape

**Width:**
- [ ] Single car: 10-12 feet
- [ ] Double car: 20-24 feet
- [ ] Triple car: 30-36 feet

**Length:**
- [ ] Per vehicle: 18-22 feet
- [ ] Turnaround space: Additional 20x20 feet

**Shape:**
- [ ] Straight
- [ ] L-shaped
- [ ] Circular
- [ ] Custom

### Turnaround Space

**If needed:**
- [ ] Hammerhead (T-shaped)
- [ ] Circular drive
- [ ] Y-turn area
- [ ] Connection to alternate exit

### Slope and Drainage Planning

**Grade:**
- [ ] Minimum 1% slope for drainage
- [ ] Maximum 15% for accessibility
- [ ] Direction: Away from house

**Drainage:**
- [ ] Connection to storm system
- [ ] Swales or French drains
- [ ] Permeable surface options

### Edging and Borders

**Options:**
- [ ] Concrete curbing
- [ ] Brick or paver borders
- [ ] Natural stone
- [ ] Metal edging
- [ ] None (gravel)

## Step 5 - Check Permits and Codes

### Local Building Codes

**Research requirements:**
- [ ] Minimum/maximum width
- [ ] Setback from property lines
- [ ] Slope limitations
- [ ] Material restrictions
- [ ] Thickness requirements

### HOA Restrictions

**If applicable:**
- [ ] Approved materials list
- [ ] Color restrictions
- [ ] Design requirements
- [ ] Application process

### Utility Line Locations

**Before digging:**
- [ ] Call 811 (2-3 days before)
- [ ] Mark underground lines
- [ ] Identify potential conflicts
- [ ] Plan around obstacles

## Step 6 - Hire the Right Contractor

### What to Look For

**Qualifications:**
- [ ] Licensed and insured
- [ ] Local experience
- [ ] Material specialization
- [ ] Positive references
- [ ] Portfolio of completed work

### Questions to Ask

**Essential questions:**
- [ ] How long have you been in business?
- [ ] Can you provide references?
- [ ] What's included in the quote?
- [ ] What's the timeline?
- [ ] What warranty do you offer?
- [ ] Who will supervise the work?

### Red Flags to Avoid

**Warning signs:**
- [ ] No physical business address
- [ ] Requests large upfront payment
- [ ] Pressure to sign immediately
- [ ] Significantly lower bid than others
- [ ] No proof of insurance
- [ ] Poor communication

### Getting Multiple Quotes

**Recommended:**
- [ ] Get 3-5 quotes minimum
- [ ] Compare apples-to-apples
- [ ] Check references for each
- [ ] Verify licenses and insurance
- [ ] Review contracts carefully

## Step 7 - Prepare for Installation

### Timeline Expectations

**Typical schedules:**
- [ ] Asphalt: 1-2 days
- [ ] Concrete: 3-7 days
- [ ] Pavers: 3-5 days
- [ ] Gravel: 1-2 days

**Curing times:**
- [ ] Asphalt: 3-5 days before use
- [ ] Concrete: 7 days for vehicles, 28 days full cure
- [ ] Pavers: Immediate use

### Site Access Requirements

**Prepare for:**
- [ ] Equipment delivery and staging
- [ ] Material delivery (truck access)
- [ ] Temporary parking arrangements
- [ ] Protection of landscaping

### Weather Considerations

**Ideal conditions:**
- [ ] Temperature: 50-80°F
- [ ] No rain forecast (3 days)
- [ ] Low wind
- [ ] Proper ground conditions (not frozen, not saturated)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I consider before building a driveway?

**Key considerations:** Vehicle needs, budget, climate, maintenance preferences, local codes, and contractor selection. Use this checklist to ensure nothing is overlooked.

### How do I plan a new driveway?

**Planning steps:** Define needs, set budget, choose material, design layout, check permits, hire contractor, and prepare for installation. Each step is detailed in this checklist.

### What permits do I need for a new driveway?

**Typically required:** Building permit from local municipality. May also need HOA approval and curb cut permit. Always call 811 before digging.

### How do I choose between driveway materials?

**Decision factors:** Budget, climate, maintenance preference, aesthetic goals, and expected vehicle loads. Compare options using the comparison chart in this guide.
