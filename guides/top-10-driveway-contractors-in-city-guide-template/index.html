Template Criteria: How We Rank the Best Driveway Contractors

Before you drop company names into the template, score each candidate on the same 10-point scale. Consistency keeps your list credible and your driveway drama-free.

1. Valid [State] License & City Permit History

Verify the license number on your state’s contractor board site. Bonus points if the company pulls municipal permits without you asking.

2. Proof of Insurance & Bonding

Ask for a certificate of insurance emailed directly from their agent. Minimum $1 M general liability + workers’ comp if they have employees.

3. Driveway Specialization Years

We filter for teams that devote ≥60 % of annual revenue to flat-work (concrete, asphalt, pavers). General landscapers who “also do driveways” don’t make our cut.

4. Local Project Gallery & References

Look for 15+ completed jobs within a 15-mile radius of [City]. Drive by two at random; knock on the homeowner’s door—most people love to brag (or complain) about their new driveway.

5. Online Rating Trend (Not Just Stars)

We average Google, Yelp, and Nextdoor ratings over the last 12 months, then deduct a full point if there’s a pattern of identical one-liner reviews (red flag for fake feedback).

6. Material Warranties & Workmanship Guarantees

Minimum 2-year workmanship warranty on concrete, 3-year on asphalt. Pavers should include manufacturer’s lifetime structural warranty.

7. Transparent Estimates & Payment Schedule

Itemized bids that break out demo, base, steel, concrete PSI, sealer, and tax score higher. We flag any request for >50 % down.

8. Lead Times & Crew Size

Top contractors in [City] book 2–6 weeks out in spring. We give preference to companies that own enough trucks & mixers to avoid weather delays.

9. Sustainable Options Offered

Permeable pavers, recycled asphalt, or low-VOC sealers earn extra credit as more [City] homeowners face storm-water runoff rules.

10. Community Presence & Awards

BBB A+, Angi Super Service, or local Chamber “Small Business of the Year” signals stability and accountability.

How to Research Each Contractor in 15 Minutes

Step 1: Batch-Search License & Insurance

Open the state contractor board site in one tab and your spreadsheet in another. Knock out all 10 license checks before moving on—multitasking kills accuracy.

Step 2: Use Google Maps “Photos” Layer

Search “driveway installation near me,” click a company, then scroll to customer-uploaded photos. Dated photos reveal seasonal quality; look for cracks after one winter.

Step 3: Skim the 3-Star Reviews First

Five-star reviews gush; one-star rants. Three-star reviews often contain balanced pros & cons (“great crew, but sealer peeled”). You’ll spot recurring issues fast.

Step 4: Check Court Records

Your county clerk’s online docket usually allows free name searches. Zero judgments = peace of mind.

10 Must-Ask Questions Before You Hire

  1. Will you pull the driveway permit, and is the fee in this bid?
  2. What PSI slump for concrete, and what reinforcement mesh or rebar spacing?
  3. How many inches of compacted base will you install, and what aggregate size?
  4. Do you use wood or metal edge forms, and will they stay until full cure?
  5. When will expansion joints be cut (time & depth)?
  6. What brand of sealer, and how soon after pour will it be applied?
  7. How long must I keep vehicles off the driveway?
  8. Is the quoted thickness measured at the center or edges?
  9. What happens if the weather drops below 40 °F during cure?
  10. Will the crew dispose of old concrete, and is tonnage capped?

Print this list; any hesitation or vague answers are automatic red flags.

Maintenance Tips to Keep Your New Driveway Looking New

Concrete

  • Wait 30 days, then apply silane-siloxane sealer; reseal every 3–5 years
  • Use a concrete-safe ice melt (calcium magnesium acetate)
  • Pressure-wash at 2,500 PSI max, keeping wand 12″ from surface

Asphalt

  • Seal-coat 6–12 months after install, then every 2–3 years
  • Fill cracks <¼ in. with rubberized crack filler each spring
  • Avoid gasoline & oil spills; use cat litter to absorb quickly

Pavers

  • Sweep polymeric sand into joints every 2 years to block weeds
  • Apply matte paver sealer to darken color & stabilize sand
  • Rotate stained pavers individually—no saw-cutting needed
