Resin vs Block Paving Driveway: The 2024 Homeowner’s Guide

Choosing a new driveway surface can feel like picking a new phone—every option promises the world, but only one truly fits your lifestyle. In the red corner we have resin bound surfacing: sleek, seamless and SUDS-compliant. In the blue corner sits block paving: classic, modular and time-tested. Below we unpack every angle—costs, looks, installation, upkeep, drainage, lifespan and eco-impact—so you can make a confident, future-proof decision.

Up-Front Costs: What You’ll Actually Pay in 2024

Prices below are for a standard 50 m² (540 ft²) two-car driveway in the Midwest, including excavation, edging and labor. Add 10–15 % for coastal cities, subtract 5 % for rural areas.

  • Resin bound: $10–$16 per sq ft installed ($18–$29 per m²)
  • Concrete block paving: $8–$14 per sq ft installed ($15–$25 per m²)

Hidden Extras That Pop Up Later

Resin needs a solid base—usually fiber-reinforced concrete or tarmac. If your old driveway is crumbling, budget an extra $3–$4 per sq ft for base work. Block paving can often overlay a stable concrete pad, but you’ll still pay for kiln-dried sand and a compactor. Always insist on a fixed-item quote so surprises stay off the invoice.

Installation Speed & Disruption

Resin: One-Day Wonder

Prep work aside (base laying, edging, crack repair), the actual resin pour takes 3–4 hours on a 50 m² drive. You can walk on it in 6 hours and park on it in 24—great if you hate being displaced.

Block Paving: Slower But Sectional

Expect 2–3 days for excavation, sub-base, screeding and laying. The bonus: installers can work one section at a time, so you still have partial access each night.

Design & Curb Appeal

Resin: Seamless Color Palette

Choose from 40+ natural aggregates and RAL color tints. You can inset decorative borders, house numbers or even glow-in-the-dark stone for a modern twist. The smooth finish flatters contemporary homes and adds perceived value to modern builds.

Block Paving: Classic Patterns

Herringbone, basket-weave, stretcher-bond—the choice is yours. Bricks come in 3 sizes, 20 colors and multiple textures (smooth, tumbled, stone-effect). Ideal for heritage or colonial façades.

Durability & Load-Bearing

Resin: Flexible But Not Invincible

A correctly laid 18 mm resin surface on a concrete base handles 7,500 lb passenger vehicles with ease. Avoid dragging trailer jacks or motorcycle stands—point loads can dent the UV-stable polyurethane. Repair is simple: cut out, re-prime, re-pour.

Block Paving: Individual Brick Strength

Concrete blocks (8,000 psi+) distribute weight laterally. You can replace single bricks after heavy forklift traffic or oil spills. Use sealant every 4 years to prevent color fade and efflorescence.

Day-to-Day Maintenance Compared

Task Resin Block Paving
Sweeping Monthly Monthly
Power-wash 1× year (low pressure) 2× year + re-sand joints
Weed control Minimal Bi-annual kiln-dried sand
Reseal Optional clear coat every 5 yrs Color seal every 3–4 yrs

Pro tip: Use a plastic-bladed shovel on resin to avoid scuffing, and scatter play sand on block paving in icy weather—rock salt eats concrete faces over time.

Rain, Snow & Drainage Regulations

Resin: SUDS-Compliant by Design

Bound resin is porous—water drains through at 850 gal/m²/hr. That usually eliminates the need for planning permission, even in front-yard installations. Pair it with a permeable open-graded base and you’re fully compliant with EPA storm-water rules.

Block Paving: Permeable Options Exist

Standard concrete blocks are NOT porous; you need a gradient + channel drain. Alternatively, specify permeable blocks and a 6 in clean-stone sub-base. Cost rises 15 % but you stay code-legal.

Climate & Color Fading

UV-Stable Resin

Aliphatic polyurethane binders won’t yellow under intense sun. Dark aggregates can lighten half a shade over 7–8 years, but it’s uniform—no patchiness.

Concrete Block Pigment

Iron-oxide pigments are colorfast for ~10 years. After that, surface wear reveals the natural concrete gray. A fresh coat of breathable sealer restores depth for about $0.60 per sq ft in materials.

Eco Footprint & Recyclability

Resin uses quarried aggregate shipped regionally, plus 3–4 % petro-based PU. Some suppliers now offer bio-resin with 35 % renewable content—ask for REACH certificates. Old resin drives can be planed off and recycled as road base.

Concrete blocks lock up 12 % recycled aggregates and can be lifted, cleaned and reused. Broken bricks head straight to crushed concrete plants—zero landfill.

DIY Potential: Can You Really Do It Yourself?

Resin: Leave It to the Pros

You have 7–12 minutes to trowel the mixed resin before it gels. Pros use forced-action mixers and gauge rakes to hit 18 mm ±2 mm. Botch the mix ratio and you’ll have sticky patches that never cure. Warranty is also void without installer certification.

Block Paving: Ambitious Weekend Project

Homeowners with a plate compactor, spirit level and patience can lay 100 sq ft per day. Rent a wet-cut saw for curves and budget 10 % extra blocks for breakages. You still need a laser level on the sub-base—get that wrong and puddles appear.

ROI & Property Value

Realtors we polled in Ohio and Texas say a new resin drive adds 6–8 % to asking price; block paving adds 5–7 %. Resin’s modern vibe appeals to buyers under 45; block paving resonates with families who like traditional charm. Either way, a tired, cracked driveway knocks 10 % off perceived value—so doing nothing costs more than upgrading.

5-Minute Decision Checklist

  1. Need fast install and minimal upkeep? → Resin
  2. Love classic patterns and easy single-brick repair? → Block
  3. Site slopes >5 % and no drains? → Permeable resin or permeable blocks
  4. Heavy RV or boat storage? → Block (80 mm thick) on 6 in concrete sub-base
  5. Historic district design rules? → Block (often mandated)
