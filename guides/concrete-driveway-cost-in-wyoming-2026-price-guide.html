Concrete Driveway Cost in Wyoming: The 2026 Big-Picture

A new concrete driveway is one of the fastest ways to boost curb appeal and stand up to Wyoming’s wild temperature swings, high winds, and road-salt brine. Before you call a contractor, it helps to know where prices are heading. In 2026, expect to pay $9.50 – $17.25 per square foot installed for a standard 4-inch-thick broom-finish driveway in most Wyoming towns. That range covers everything—tear-out of the old surface, base prep, concrete, labor, and a one-year warranty.

The final bill depends on four big levers: site conditions, design upgrades, concrete mix design, and contractor availability. Below we break each lever down so you can budget confidently and avoid “allowance creep.”

2026 Concrete Driveway Cost Breakdown for Wyoming Homeowners

1. Average Price by Project Size

Most residential driveways in Wyoming fall between 600 sq ft (single-car) and 1,200 sq ft (three-car). Use these ballpark numbers when you measure your own footprint.

  • 600 sq ft: $5,700 – $10,350
  • 900 sq ft: $8,550 – $15,525
  • 1,200 sq ft: $11,400 – $20,700

6 Hidden Factors That Raise or Lower Your Bid

1. Soil Type & Frost Depth

Clay-heavy soils in the Wind River Basin hold more water and expand 8–10 %, requiring 6 in. of engineered base instead of 4 in. Budget an extra $1.50 / sq ft if geotechnical borings show CL-ML silts.

2. Accessibility for Ready-Mix Trucks

A 1,200 ft long gravel lane in the Bighorns may need a concrete pump ($900 flat) or multiple buggy loads ($175 per hour). Mention gate widths, overhead wires, and septic locations when you request a quote.

3>3. Slope & Drainage

Driveways pitched >12 % require broomed transverse grooves for traction and a stamped “eco-swale” at the apron to meet Wyoming DEQ storm-water rules. Allow +$1.00 / sq ft.

4. Reinforcement Choices

#3 rebar on 18-in. grid adds $0.85 / sq ft; synthetic micro-fiber only $0.22 / sq ft. For heavy half-ton pickups, rebar wins every time.

5>5. Seasonal Demand

May through July is peak. Book during the February “pre-season” and you can shave 7–10 % off total cost; contractors value filling calendars early.

6. Warranty Length

A two-year warranty vs. the standard one-year adds roughly $0.40 / sq ft but includes one free joint-sealing service—usually worth it on textured finishes.

Practical Ways to Save Without Cutting Quality

  • Bundle with neighbors. Three driveways on the same block poured the same week can split a $1,200 truck pump fee and trigger 5 % volume discount on concrete.
  • Keep it standard. A broom-finish, 4-in. slab with 1-in. edge radius is 35 % cheaper than stamped ashlar slate but still lasts 30+ years when sealed.
  • Do your own saw-cut joints. Rent a 14-in. electric saw for $65/day and cut 1-in. deep joints 24 hours after pour—saves $0.65 / sq ft.
  • Reuse base. If old asphalt is <2 in. thick and drains well, some contractors will mill it into the gravel base for $0.50 / sq ft instead of full removal.
  • Seal on schedule. A $450 seal every 3 years prevents freeze-thaw spalling that costs $3,000 to patch later.

Typical Project Timeline (Weather Permitting)

  1. Mon – Tue: Site check, utility locates, permit pull
  2. Wed: Remove old surface, haul off
  3. Thu: Install gravel base, compact in lifts
  4. Fri: Place concrete, finish, cure compound
  5. Sat: Remove forms, initial clean-up
  6. 7 days later: Saw-cut joints, seal initial coat
  7. 28 days later: Light vehicle traffic; second seal optional

Cool, overcast 50 °F days are ideal; Wyoming’s 30 °F morning jumps to 75 °F afternoon can cause surface crazing. Ask your contractor to use an evaporative retarder if the forecast shows >20 °F daily swing.

How to Hire the Right Concrete Contractor in Wyoming

  1. Verify license. Wyoming doesn’t license concrete at state level, but Cheyenne, Casper, and Gillette require a city contractor card—ask for proof.
  2. Demand a mix design. Minimum 4,000 psi, 5–7 % air entrainment for freeze-thaw, 2–4 in. slump. Get it in writing.
  3. Check past pours >5 years old. Look for random cracking >1/4 in. or spalling at joints—red flags.
  4. Compare apples-to-apples bids. Ensure each quote includes tear-out, base, concrete, reinforcement, joints, sealer, and warranty.
  5. Hold 10 % retainage until 28-day inspection passes. This keeps schedules honest and gives leverage if patches are needed.
