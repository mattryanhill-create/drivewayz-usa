**Meta description:** Compare conventional, aerobic, mound, and other septic system types. Get cost estimates and factors that affect your installation or replacement project.

## H1: Septic System Types and Costs

Choosing the right septic system for your property depends on soil conditions, lot size, local codes, and budget. This guide covers the main system types and what you can expect to pay.

## Conventional Septic Systems

Conventional gravity-fed systems are the most common. They work best when soil percolates well and there’s adequate space for a drainfield. Costs typically range from $3,000 to $8,000 for a standard residential installation.

## Aerobic Treatment Systems

Aerobic systems add oxygen to improve treatment. They’re ideal for smaller lots, poor soil, or environmentally sensitive areas. Expect $10,000 to $20,000 depending on size and complexity.

## Mound and Alternative Systems

Mound systems elevate the drainfield above the natural soil and are used when the water table is high or soil is shallow. Chamber and drip distribution systems offer alternatives when traditional options aren’t feasible. Costs vary from $8,000 to $25,000.

## Factors That Affect Cost

Site conditions, permit fees, tank size, and local labor rates all influence the final price. A perc test and site evaluation will help narrow down the best option and give you a more accurate quote.
